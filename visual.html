Visual Design

Spring 2017 | Adobe Illustrator

What

A collection of growing visual creations in an attempt to sharpen my Illustrator skills while exploring art as a digital medium, many of which are an homage to my new home at Cal.

Why

Throughout high school, my interactions with visual design only manifested in roles of directing designers themselves. Although I always wanted to explore digital art in more handson ways, I never had access to Adobe products.

At Berkeley, the Student Technology Council offers free software downloads, which includes the whole Adobe Creative Suite.

No Vacation
Reflecting on the transience of the summer days leading up to the first day of my job, I vectored some popsicles and put the text "get them fresh" to remind myself that time can sometimes slip away from us.

Reflections

I didn't know what to expect when beginning Illustrator, but I didn't brace for the steep learning curve ahead. Although initially overwhelmed by the seemingly endless variations of tools and the foreign interface of Adobe products, I've begun to accept process of visual design as a very sophisticated art of clever problem-solving. I feel more confident to take on design projects, big or small.

My designs are by no means perfect, but I hope that practice and persistence will allow me to continue to grow in my skills.
